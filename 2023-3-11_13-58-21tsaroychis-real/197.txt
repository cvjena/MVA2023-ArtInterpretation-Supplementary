Charles V was an emperors of the Holy Roman Empire, which encompassed almost all of Europe at the time. He was a formidable ruler and is credited with leading the Habsburg dynasty to catastrophic success. He was also known for his patronage of the arts, which led to the flourishing of painting in the Renaissance. In this portrait, Titian captures the emperor in his regal robes, with his pet dog at his feet. The dog appears to be sympathetic and attentive to its master, while the emperor is seemingly at ease and radiates power and confidence. The background is filled with lush vegetation, indicating the magnitude of the emperor's achievements.
