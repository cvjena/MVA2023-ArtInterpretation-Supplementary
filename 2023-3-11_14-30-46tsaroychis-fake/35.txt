This painting is a religious altarpiece made for the church of San Pantaleon in Venice. It shows the Virgin Mary and baby Jesus receiving the confession of Saint Peter. The painting is on the front of a fold-out altar, and on either side are statues of saints. In the background is a colonnade, and behind the figures is an open window with the light of a stars.  This painting could symbolize the conversion of Saint Peter, who is seen here as a highly respected and powerful figure. The Virgin Mary is depicted as pious and caring, and her acceptance of Saint Peter's confession shows her faith and love.
