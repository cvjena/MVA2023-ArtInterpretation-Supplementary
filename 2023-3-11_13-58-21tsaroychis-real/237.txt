The moon is a symbol of the feminine and it has been used throughout history as a representation of the goddess. It can also symbolize the cycle of life, growth, and change. The soft, gentle light of the moon seems to etherealize the landscape, while the clouds reflect the light back giving the impression of a heavenly backdrop. It is possible that the artist was intending to create a feeling of peace and tranquility with this painting. The peaceful and moon-like backdrop gives the painting an ethereal feeling. The artist may have chosen to depict the moon as a representation of the goddess in the light of day. This painting could be seen as a symbol of female vitality and power.
