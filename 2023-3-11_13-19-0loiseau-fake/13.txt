The painting is predominantly a blue has a yellow tinge to it, with white highlights in the sky, the sun and the buildings. Much of the painting is reflective, giving the impression of glass. There are a few people kayaking, seeming to be in a serene, peaceful setting. The brushstrokes are simple and graceful, giving the painting enough depth to give it a feel of depth. The painting could be interpreted as a depiction of the natural beauty of the United States, or of the utopian potential of the New York Cityscape. It could also be seen as a commentary on the postwar years, with revelry and hope underlying in the bright colors.
