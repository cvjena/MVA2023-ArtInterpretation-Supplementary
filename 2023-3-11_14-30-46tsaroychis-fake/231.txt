Durer composed this painting in 1514 and it reflects his personal beliefs about Christianity. The main figure is symbolically representing Jesus Christ and the surrounding landscape represents the events leading to Jesus's crucifixion. Christ's figure dominates the painting with its powerful pose and commanding presence. The surrounding landscape is full of symbolism referencing different biblical events. The trees on the right side of the painting may represent the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil from the bible, while the mountains in the background could represent Mount Calvary where Jesus was crucified. The angels in the sky could represent God's protection. The other figures in the painting could represent various aspects of Jesus's life such as his birth, his death and his resurrection.
