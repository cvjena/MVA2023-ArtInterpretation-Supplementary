The painting is a study of land and sky, depicting the Earth as it might look from an high altitude. The deep blue of the sky contrasts with the sharply delineated, contrasting shades of green and brown, while the land appears as a dark expanse below. Pasmore believed that depicting natural scenes with accurate colors and tones was essential to the depiction of the natural world, in order to bring attention to the elemental qualities of the landscape. In this painting, the harmony of nature is juxtaposed with the strength of the sky, hinting at the mysteries of the universe.  There are many interpretations that could be given to these paintings, as the artist's intent is unknown. These are just some possible interpretations.
